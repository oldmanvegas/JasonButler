ABOUT

Jason Butler is a slam winning spoken word poet who loves performing at venues and festivals as either headline act, eager open-mic participant, slam entrant or host.

His work spans a plethora of topics from the emotional confessional, the occasional rant, grossly exaggerated anecdotes through to stand up silliness. Also, if asked nicely he can merrily weave an improvised verse from thin air.

Based in Cornwall, he arrived oven-ready on the poetry scene in 2014 after two decades performing as a renowned hiphop MC under his alter ego VEGAS.

His first collection of poetry,
"Miscellany of One"
was published in December 2023 and is available now.

As well as his poetry performances he's currently frontman for the bands Antimatador and Freshly Squeezed performing on stages all over the UK and beyond.

COMMISSIONED POETRY

A selection of poems written specifically for client projects.

We're Still Here

This short film was produced by Rame Conservation Trust and Maker Memories to mark the centenary of Maker Camp 1923-2023.

The film traces the social and cultural history of the site from 1923 onwards showing how the past is inextricably linked to the present via a series of threads: connecting people to nature; art; music and well-being.

This film was selected to be part of the Cornwall 2023 Film Festival in the short film category.

Filmed and edited by Tom Kirkman, poem recorded at Patchwork Studios, Maker Heights.

We Do Not Fear the Dark

Written to capture the atmosphere of the Flaming Tar Barrels of Ottery St Mary for this mesmeric short film by Amanda Bluglass and Danny Cooke.

This short film was selected as a finalist in the national Outspoken Prize for Poetry in the "Poetry for Film" category.

Narrated by Tim McGill

A Taste of the English Riviera

Written to capture the fishing industry of the English Riviera, to highlight the connection from sea to plate. Caught in this short film by Danny Cooke.

Narrated by Tim McGill
